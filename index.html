Airplane General

Runway slope +/– 2%

  • Takeoff and landing max. tailwind:
  • All 737-800 with winglets
    • 12 kts. with flap 30
    • 15 kts. with flap 40
  • All other 737s
    • 15 kts tailwind

Maximum speeds - different for each individual: Observe gear and flap placards

Maximum Operating Altitude: 41,000 ft

Maximum Takeoff and Landing Altitude: 8,400 ft

Non–AFM Operational Information

  • Crosswind - demonstrated (check operations manual and aircraft manual):

    • Dry/wet runway 36/25kts, winglets 33/25kts.


    HGS (HUD) operation:

    • Headwind 25 kts.
    • Crosswind 15 kts.
    • Tailwind 10 kts.

    Do not operate HF radios (if installed) during refuelling operations.

  • Altitude Display Limits for RVSM Operations
  • Standby altimeters do not meet altimeter accuracy requirements of RVSM airspace.
  • The maximum allowable in-flight difference between Captain and First Officer altitude displays for RVSM operations is 200 feet.

Air Systems

  • Pressurization
  • The maximum cabin differential pressure (relief valves) is 9.1 psi.

Autopilot/Flight Director System

  • Use of aileron trim with the autopilot engaged is prohibited.
  • Do not engage the autopilot for takeoff below 400 feet AGL.
  • Airplanes operating under JAA Rules: The autopilot must be disengaged before the airplane descends more than 50 feet below the minimum descent altitude (MDA) unless it is coupled to an ILS glide slope and localizer or in the go-around mode.
  • 737-600
  • Airplanes operating under JAA Rules: The Minimum Use Height (MUH) for single channel autopilot operation is defined as 138 feet AGL.
  • 737-700
  • Airplanes operating under JAA Rules: The Minimum Use Height (MUH) for single channel autopilot operation is defined as 135 feet AGL.
  • 737-800
  • Airplanes operating under JAA Rules: The Minimum Use Height (MUH) for single channel autopilot operation is defined as 158 feet AGL.
  • Airplanes operating under JAA Rules: Maximum allowable wind speeds, when conducting a dual channel Cat II or Cat III landing predicated on autoland operations, are:
    • Headwind 25 knots
    • Crosswind 20 knots
  • Maximum and minimum glideslope angles for autoland are 3.25 degrees and 2.5 degrees respectively.
  • Autoland capability may only be used with flaps 30 or 40 and both engines operative.

Engines and APU

  • Engine Ignition
  • Engine ignition must be on for:
    • takeoff
    • landing
    • operation in heavy rain
    • anti-ice operation.
  • Reverse Thrust
  • Intentional selection of reverse thrust in flight is prohibited.
  • APU
  • Airplanes operating under JAA Rules: APU bleed + electrical load: max alt 10,000 ft.
  • APU bleed: max alt 17,000 ft.
  • APU electrical load: max alt 41,000 ft.

Flight Controls

  • Max flap extension altitude is 20,000 ft.
  • Holding in icing conditions with flaps extended is prohibited.
  • Do not deploy the speedbrakes in flight at radio altitudes less than 1,000 feet.
  • In flight, do not extend the SPEED BRAKE lever beyond the FLIGHT DETENT.
  • Avoid rapid and large alternating control inputs, especially in combination with large changes in pitch, roll, or yaw (e.g. large side slip angles) as they may result in structural failure at any speed, including below VA.

Fuel System

  • Maximum tank fuel temperature: 49°C.
  • Minimum inflight tank fuel temperature: 3°C above the freezing point of the fuel being used or -43°C, whichever is higher.
  • Fuel Balance
  • Lateral imbalance between main tanks 1 and 2 must be scheduled to be zero.
  • Random fuel imbalance must not exceed 453 kgs for taxi, takeoff, flight or landing.
  • Fuel Loading
  • Main tanks 1 and 2 must be full if center tank contains more than 453 kgs.

Landing Gear

Operation with assumed temperature reduced takeoff thrust is not permitted with anti-skid inoperative.

Other Notes

PF loads all flight data, route, forecast winds, cost index etc. into the FMC. The CDU preflight is at this stage only to be completed up to and including the PERF INIT page (The remaining 2 pages N1 Limit and Take Off REF are left blank and will be filled out after completion of the TODC (Take-Off Data Calculation) procedure).

FMC Route check and ACARS initialization:

PF enters the route in the FMC but does not activate it.

PM must verify that the route in the FMC is correct. This is done by checking the FMC RTE page vs. the OFP route.

After activation of the route PM can now initialize the flight in the ACARS system with date/flight number.

Wind:

Initially insert the TOC wind on the PERF INIT page as this will determine your climb speed. After PM has checked and executed the route you should enter the average wind component on the LEGS / RTE DATA page (the RTE DATA prompt is not available until the route has been activated and executed).

Briefings

The descent procedure and checklist should be completed before initiating the descent. This includes possible “early” descents due to ATC-requirements.
Perform Approach Briefing including the following:
  • Fuel status
    • Review fuel status with regards to missed approach and diversion to an alternate.
  • Technical status
    • Review aircraft technical status for items affecting approach and landing.
  • NOTAMs
    • Brief on relevant NOTAMs.
  • Weather
    • Decide on minimum weather conditions applicable for the type of approach to be made.
  • Use of automation and flap setting
    • Decide and brief on appropriate use of AFS, landing flaps and auto brakes. Determine stopping distance.
  • Clearance limits
    • Advise PNF to warn whenever the applicable clearance limit has been reached.
  • Altitudes
    • Decide on applicable MSA, IAA, DA/DH or MA/MDA and altitudes over approach fixes pointed out on the IAL.
  • Runway exit point
    • To achieve minimum runway occupancy time (MROT), the expected runway exit point. The rapid exit taxiway (RET), should be included in the approach briefing when operating to an aerodrome with high intensity runway operation (HIRO). Check Flap and autobrake setting to be commensurate with the approximate distance required for this.
  • Taxiing
    • Brief on applicable airport and taxi charts.
  • Look out
    • State who is to stay on instruments and who is to look out for visual guidance after
    • "Approaching minima” has been called.
  • Low visibility procedures – CAT II/III approaches
    • Brief on all relevant call outs and procedures before commencing a CAT II/III approach and landing.
  • RNAV approaches
    • Brief on all relevant call outs before commencing an RNAV approach.
  • Go-around and alternates
    • Regardless of the actual weather conditions, always ensure that the missed approach procedure, including setting of navigation aids, is fully understood. When applicable, brief relevant aspects regarding the chosen alternate.
  • Special conditions and operations including possible threats
    • E.g. crew familiarization with the route or airport flown, environmental, non-standard noise abatement, mountainous terrain, airspace constraints etc.).

FMC

The descent procedure and checklist should be completed before initiating the descent. This includes possible “early” descents due to ATC-requirements.
  • FMC descent page; Check 250 kts below FL100 (note speed should not exceed 250 kts below FL50)
  • FMC Forecast page; Descent winds, QNH and Anti-ice if appropriate
  • FMC DEP/ARR pages; Dheck correct STAR/IAL procedure selected
  • FMC Legs page; Check routings, speeds and altitude requirements
  • Set NAV display to Plan Mode so as to have better indication of route being flown
